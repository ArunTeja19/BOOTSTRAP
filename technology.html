Fusion Propulsion Deep Dive
Project Goal:

To reduce transit time to Mars from months to weeks by generating massive thrust efficiency through controlled nuclear fusion reactions.

Technical Breakdown:
  • Fuel Type: Deuterium/Helium-3 or Deuterium/Tritium plasma.
  • Confinement: Magnetic nozzle for directing plasma exhaust.
  • TRL Status: Currently restricted to ground-based laboratory feasibility demonstrations (TRL 2-3).

Current Challenge: Achieving sustained, positive net power output in a compact, flight-ready reactor.

Artificial Gravity Habitat Schematic
Project Goal:

Develop large, rotating structures to simulate partial gravity (e.g., 0.3g for Mars missions) to protect astronaut health during long-duration voyages.

Technical Breakdown:
  • Method: Centrifugal force generated by rotation.
  • Key Issue: Managing Coriolis effects on astronaut balance and internal liquid systems.
  • Design Concept: Toroidal or "dumbbell" configurations for maximizing habitat radius.
In-Orbit Manufacturing Report
Project Goal:

Reduce mission reliance on Earth resupply by manufacturing tools, components, and even structural elements in space.

Technical Breakdown:
  • Printer Types: Fused Deposition Modeling (FDM) and metallic printers.
  • Material Behavior: Studying printing process anomalies due to microgravity environment.
  • Applications: Manufacturing replacement parts for ISS and future Martian habitats.
Ion Propulsion: Xenon Thrusters
Project Goal:

Achieve extremely high fuel efficiency (Isp) for uncrewed deep space probes that require low but sustained acceleration over years.

Technical Breakdown:
  • Principle: Ionizing Xenon gas and accelerating the ions using an electric field.
  • Thrust: Very low (grams of force) but constant.
  • Key Missions: NASA's Dawn, Hayabusa (JAXA), and Psyche missions.
